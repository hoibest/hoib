Uveitis
Classification Criteria for
Punctate Inner Choroiditis
Should satisfy #1 AND #2

  1. Multifocal choroidal inflammatory lesions
    1. Predominant lesion size < 250 µm AND
    2. Punctate lesion appearance

  2. Lesion involvement of posterior pole with or without mid-periphery

  3. Absent to minimal anterior chamber and vitreous inflammation

Exclude if any criterion below is fulfilled

  • Positive serology for syphilis using a treponemal test
  • Evidence of sarcoidosis (either bilateral hilar adenopathy on chest imaging or tissue biopsy demonstrating non-caseating granulomata)
Standardization of Uveitis Nomenclature Working Group. Classification criteria for punctate inner choroiditis. Am J Ophthalmol 2021;228:275-80.
