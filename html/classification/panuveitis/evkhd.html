Uveitis
Classification Criteria for
Early-Stage Vogt-Koyanagi-Harada Disease
Should satisfy (#1 OR #2) AND #3

  1. Evidence of Harada's disease
    1. Serous (exudative) retinal detachment AND (b. and/or c.)
    2. Multi-loculated appearance on fluorescein angiogram OR
    3. Septae on optical coherence tomogram

  2. Panuveitis* with >2 of the following neurologic symptoms or signs†
    1. Headache OR
    2. Tinnitus OR
    3. Dysacusis OR
    4. Meningismus OR
    5. Cerebrospinal fluid pleocytosis

  3. No history of penetrating ocular trauma or vitreoretinal surgery prior to disease onset

Exclude if any criterion below is fulfilled

  • Positive serology for syphilis using a treponemal test
  • Evidence for sarcoidosis (either bilateral hilar adenopathy on chest imaging or tissue biopsy demonstrating non-caseating granulomata)
*Uveitis should have evidence of choroidal involvement on clinical examination, fluorescein angiography, indocyanine green angiography, or optical coherence tomography, including enhanced depth imaging of the choroid. †Onset of neurologic symptoms and signs and onset of the uveitis should occur within 4 weeks of each other.

References
